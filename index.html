Features

INDUSTRIAL QUALITY ARMS

A robot's arms are its means of interaction with the outside world. They're how robots accomplish useful tasks, and so it's essential for researchers and educators alike to have high quality yet accessible arms.

Precise, durable, fast, human-sized, and modular. These are the qualities we set out to achieve with Apollo. Each of the 5-DOF + gripper arms has the same arm reach as a human, covering an impressive two cubic meters in operational workspace. Precision and repeatability is on the millimeter scale, to enable precise manipulation tasks. And with a 1.3 m/s speed and 1.8 kg payload capacity at full arm extension, Apollo can be used to tackle real robotic applications.

SOFTWARE

Hardware is only as useful as its underlying software. The Big Solve API gives developers and researchers easy access to all parts of the robot, from reading sensor data to moving individual arm joints, to recording, saving, and playing back sequences of joint motions. Currently available in a convenient and lightweight Python library. Apollo also comes stocked with ROS integration.

SENSORS

The future of robot sensing and control is going to be vision based, and to that end we've equipped Apollo with the powerful Microsoft Kinect 2.0, which features state-of-the-art depth sensing technology and a high-res RGB camera. Additional sensor upgrades are available, including joint rotary encoders, and joint force sensing.

Platform

What really makes Apollo stand out is the seamless integration between high quality hardware, powerful open source software, and the inherent modularity. We designed Apollo from the ground up to be portable as well, with easy grip side mounted handles. The platform comes in a simple and fun build-it-yourself kit, LEGO-style.

Flexible Financing

Our mission is to enable as many researchers and educators as possible to join the robotics revolution. While we've been able to create an incredibly capable and affordable platform, our efforts don't stop there. Both individuals and institutions can request to join a flexible monthly lease program, starting at only $195/mo.

Open Source

Every part of [PLATFORM_NAME] is open source and yours to discover (and modify!). From the hardware assembly guide to the Big Solve API to the embedded motor driver software, we are committed to having every aspect of our platform be open source. We want to work with the community and leverage the combined expertise of people from around the world to kick start the robotics revolution.

Modularity

We want our robots to be fully extensible and hackable. Everything from additional joint sensors to different gripper mechanisms will be available. And we invite you in the community to contribute to the platform. Design and share new grippers, sensor modules, and more!
