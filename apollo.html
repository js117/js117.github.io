Features

INDUSTRIAL QUALITY ARMS

A robot's arms are its means of interaction with the outside world. They're how robots accomplish useful tasks, and so it's essential for researchers and educators alike to have a platform high quality arms.

Precise, durable, fast, human-sized, and modular. These are the qualities we set out to achieve with Apollo. Each of the 6-DOF + gripper arms has the same arm reach as a human, covering an impressive two cubic meters in operational workspace. Precision and repeatability is on the millimeter scale, to enable precise manipulation tasks. And with over 1 m/s speed and a whopping 20 lbs max payload capacity at full arm extension, Apollo can be used to tackle real industry applications.

SOFTWARE

The Big Solve API gives users easy access to all parts of the robot: reading sensor data, controlling arm joints, and recording, saving, and play-back of motion sequences. Currently available in a convenient and lightweight Python library. Apollo can also work with the ROS library.

Researchers and developers will also have access to the Big Solve Portal, an online marketplace where you can share your robot application software! At Big Solve our mission is to create a free and open global R&D center for robotics and artificial intelligence.

SENSORS

Vision and depth sensing are going to have a huge role in the future of robotics, and to that end we've equipped Apollo with the powerful Microsoft Kinect 2.0, which features state-of-the-art depth sensing technology and a high-res RGB camera. Also included are joint rotary encoders. Additional upgrade options are available, such as joint force sensing.

Platform

What really makes Apollo stand out is the seamless integration between high quality hardware, powerful open source software, and the inherent modularity built-in. We designed Apollo from the ground up to be portable as well, with available accessories such as mobile stands and lifting handles. Apollo is truly collaborative, not just the software but the hardware as well: modular end-effector upgrades mean you'll be able to mount custom tools such as drills, cutters, soldering equipment, and even welding gear.

Flexible Financing

Our mission is to enable as many researchers and educators as possible to join the robotics revolution. While we've been able to create an incredibly capable and affordable platform, our efforts don't stop there. We have semester-long rental programs, as well as monthly lease options. Please contact us for more information.

Open Source

Every part of Apollo is open source and yours to discover (and modify!). From the end-effector to the Big Solve API to the embedded motor driver software, we are committed to having as much of our platform be open source as possible. We want to work with the community and leverage the combined expertise of people from around the world to kick start the robotics revolution.

Modularity

We want our robots to be fully extensible and hackable. Everything from additional joint sensors to different gripper mechanisms will be available. And we invite you in the community to contribute to the platform. Design and share new grippers, sensor modules, and more! For custom requests, please contact us.

FAQ

Below are answers to the most frequently asked questions we have received.

Q: What is the deadline to apply to the beta program trial?
A: Beta trials are on-going, with applications open until June, 2017.

Q: Do I have to be affiliated with an institution or can I apply as an individual/hobbyists?
A: Absolutely you can! However, due to the volume of applicants we will have to carefully select where we deploy our early version hardware.

Q: How does Pre-Ordering work?
A: Pre-Orders are for the purpose of locking in your commitment for our official launch. Think of it as saving your spot in line for our first production run. We require a 10% down-payment on each robot, and are currently limiting the maximum pre-order quantity to 25 robots per customer. Note that pre-orders are completely protected and refunded if products are not delivered.

Q: When will the first products be shipped?
A: September 1st, 2017. Note that we will be delivering beta versions at an on-going basis during the beta trials currently underway.

Q: Are specifications subject to change?
A: Between the beta trial and final launch, we may make minor modifications to Apollo's rated specifications. These would include small percentage changes in various quantities as we test and refine, and thus shouldn't differ to the point where they affect usage in any significantly way. In fact we expect a few parameters to improve!

Q: How much does the robot weigh? How do I move and mount it?
A: Apollo weighs approximately 140 lbs. Handles will be available to attach to the sides of the robot, and we've found that it is easily portable around the lab using a furniture dolly. The base of the robot is very sturdy and designed to limit workplace vibrations. Early testers have put Apollo everywhere from tabletops, to lab benches, to mobile platforms.

Q: Where is your company based? Can I visit?
A: Big Solve Robotics was founded by James Schuback, and currently operates in Toronto, Canada. Feel free to reach out if you're in the area - james@bigsolverobotics.com
